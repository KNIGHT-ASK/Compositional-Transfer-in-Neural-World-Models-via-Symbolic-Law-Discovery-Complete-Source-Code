\begin{letter}{To the Editors of the \\ Journal of Machine Learning Research (JMLR)}

\opening{Dear Editors,}

I am pleased to submit our manuscript, \textbf{``Compositional Transfer in Neural World Models via Symbolic Law Discovery,''} for consideration as a Research Paper in the \emph{Journal of Machine Learning Research}.

This work addresses a foundational challenge in Artificial General Intelligence: the ability of neural world models to internalize and compose modular physical invariants. While contemporary architectures excel at interpolating within trained distributions, they fundamentally lack the capacity for zero-shot composition---the algebraic superposition of independently learned dynamics to predict unseen multi-physics environments.

\textbf{Core Contributions and Novelty:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Framework of Tangent-Space Superposition:} We demonstrate that by framing learning as the prediction of tangent-space residuals, standard neural networks can internalize isolated physical laws that compose algebraically with nearly zero error in target environments never encountered during training.
    \item \textbf{Causal Discovery of Gradient Interference:} Using a novel gradient alignment diagnostic, we provide empirical proof of why monolithic scaling fails in high dimensions. We identify a state of \emph{Active Gradient Conflict} ($\rho \approx -0.99$) between competing physical tasks, establishing modularity not as a preference, but as a mathematical necessity for physical world modeling.
    \item \textbf{Performance and Rigor:} Our framework benchmarks at $99.25\%$ accuracy in symbolic law recovery (via SINDy) and achieves a \textbf{6.5x lower trajectory MSE} than monolithic baselines on chaotic 12D orbital manifolds.
    \item \textbf{Benchmark vs. Neural Operators:} We provide a head-to-head comparison with Physics-Informed Neural Operators (PINO), proving our framework is \textbf{2.5x faster} and uniquely capable of additive operator composition where PINO-style solution operators fail.
\end{enumerate}

Given the intersection of causal representation learning, scientific machine learning, and symbolic identification, we believe this manuscript is highly suited for JMLR. We provide an open-source ``Elite Validation Suite'' to ensure the full reproducibility of all reported metrics.
